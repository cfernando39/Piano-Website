About Me

Music has always been a part of my life. Many of my family members are also involved with music either with singing, or playing an instrument. I started taking piano lessons when I was seven years old, and continued taking them for 12 years. I studied at a variety of music schools including Arcadia Academy of Music, Merriam School of Music, as well as with private piano instructors.

After completing several RCM piano exams and having over 12 years of playing experience, I felt confident enough to start teaching piano. I have now been teaching piano privately for 5 years.Read the full text...

Although I started teaching piano in-person, I have been able to adapt and continuing teaching piano online through Zoom and a platform called LearnCube. I will continue teaching online until it is safe to move back to in-person classes.

In addition to playing the piano, I also play the alto saxophone, guitar and sing. I started learning the alto saxophone once I was accepted into the Regional Arts Program at St. Elizabeth Catholic High School. I enjoyed it so much that I continued playing the saxophone during my studies at the University of Toronto within the Music Education Program. The alto saxophone was my main instrument where I studied with Chelsea Shanoff and Wallace Halladay.

I graduated with a Bachelors of Music in 2019 and began Teachers College in the Fall of 2020 at OISE (Ontario Institute for Studies in Education). I hope to complete my Masters degree in 2022 and be a high school music teacher.
